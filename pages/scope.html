3. Scope

Scopes are created by functions. In modern Javascript, scopes are also created by statements such as if, for loops, while and curly brackets.

Scope determines the visiblity of a variable. Variables declared inside a function are not visible outside the function's scope. These variable are called local variables.

However, the function has access to the outer variables (from its outer scopes) and can modify their values.

Every script has a main scope known as the global scope. Every other scope is a direct or indirect children of that scope.
